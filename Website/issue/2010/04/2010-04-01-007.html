Celebrate diversity on April 17th

Public Announcement April 2010

On Saturday, April 17, the Minnesota State University Student Association (MSUSA) and Student Senate present Coexist, Celebrate and Empower, an event centered on diversity within the school and community. This all-day event, located at Metropolitan State University’s Saint Paul campus, will feature keynote speeches throughout the day by President Sue K. Hammersmith, Interim Dean of Students Cecilia Stanton and St. Paul Councilmember Melvin Carter III.

Tou Ger Xiong, the first Hmong-American comedian, rap artist and storyteller will start out the day with a performance after the breakfast. Student organizations will have booths set up from 9 a.m. to 2:30 p.m. in the Founder’s Hall reception area, offering information about their groups and answering any questions you may have.

The day is capped off by Mixed Blood Theater’s performance of Hijab Tube, presented by Seema Sueko, co-founder of the Mo`olelo Performing Arts Company in San Diego. Hijab Tube explores the issue American-Muslim identity by highlighting a young Muslim woman, Rubiya, and her own questions of identity. She poses questions regarding this issue via YouTube, eliciting a wide range of responses from other American Muslims.

All students, faculty and staff are invited to attend this fun and exciting event. Breakfast will be served in the morning and for lunch attendees can enjoy a delicious buffet that features a diverse range of meal choices. To add to the excitement, door prizes will also be awarded throughout the day. For questions about the event, please contact Mala Singh at singma@go.metrostate.edu.
