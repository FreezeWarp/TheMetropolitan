Student Life to recognize student groups, leaders

Blaine Huberty DECEMBER 2010

The Student Life and Leadership Department (SLLD) has long recognized outstanding student groups and leaders, going back to the President’s Student Leadership Awards starting in 1999. These awards, however (which take place in the spring), don’t highlight the good work done by the various student organizations and leaders as well as Student Life would like.

Starting in the next few months, SLLD is enacting changes to recognize leaders and or groups for their work monthly with a small token of appreciation.

Philip Fuehrer of Student Senate activities graciously answered questions on the new plans taking shape to further recognize the efforts of student groups and their leaders:

When did the idea first come to you to recognize student groups and leaders?
We have actually recognized student groups and individual leaders since 1999 via our annual President’s Student Leadership Awards dinner. 

Why recognize groups and leaders? 
While we have the annual awards ceremony there are plenty of high-quality programs and leadership that occurs throughout the year. Unfortunately, with the annual awards in the spring, we felt that some of the events  earlier in the year get overlooked or forgotten. This is an attempt to keep more events and individuals’ achievements in the forefront.

Can a group submit itself to be considered? 
We’re still working out final details but we’ve always allowed self-nominations in our other awards considerations.

Who selects the groups and leaders to be honored and what are the criteria by which groups and leaders are recognized?  
Again, that’s being finalized, but it’ll likely consist of a smallish panel of three or four  [judges] including the SLLD director and maybe the dean of students.

Was there any difficulty in getting the school to approve the plan, due to tight budgets or other obstacles?
No.  Other than looking to provide a small congratulatory gift of some sort—a bookstore certificate or some such thing—there is really no additional cost associated with the idea.

Who is the first group or leader that will be recognized and why?
[It’s] too soon to know.

When will they be recognized? 
Again, too soon, but we’d certainly like to have it up and running for the new year.

Will there be an actual ceremony and, if so, where will it be held? 
For these monthly awards there probably won’t be any ceremony, but winners will be automatically put into the pool of nominees for the annual awards each spring.

What are the rewards being offered? 
I can’t see the awards being anything above $50 and quite possibly in the $20 to $25 range. Simply a little bonus to say congratulations and job well done.

Is there anything else you think we should know? 
Keep your eyes open for when the program begins and be sure to nominate those you believe are deserving of the honor.
