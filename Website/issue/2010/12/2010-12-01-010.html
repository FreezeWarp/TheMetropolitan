Listen, guide and equip

Randi Johnson DECEMBER 2010

On Friday, November 19, Metropolitan State University held an open reception hosted by the Gateway Student Services Center. The purpose of the reception was to give faculty, staff and students a chance to connect with the Gateway associates and the new interim director, and to learn about the services Gateway will provide to students.

Interim Director Kelley Eubanks began the ceremony by explaining the restructuring within Gateway that has been in process since 2010. Five associates were hired and underwent an extensive four-week-long training in the areas of financial aid, records and registration, student accounts, tuition and fees and customer service.

Eubanks stressed the “top-notch, second-to-none customer service” that Gateway will offer students, including use of four kiosks in the Gateway area on the first floor of Founder’s Hall. These kiosks, Eubanks explained, will be recommended to students who want to see or update their accounts quickly.

The five Gateway associates continued the ceremony by explaining the mission statement, motto, changes in the system and the training process itself.

“[Gateway is committed to] providing timely, accurate and professional services to students,” said associate Heather Aune. Through phones, Ask Us! (the application used to communicate electronically with Gateway) and walk-in appointments, the associates follow student issues through to resolution. This includes use of investigation, professional judgment and individual discretion to serve as student advocates.

“The Gateway motto of ‘listen, guide and equip’ will be demonstrated through becoming a one-stop service center for students,” said associate Kristin Nolan. Now students can refer to one place for addressing questions regarding different departments associated with their student account.

The associates adapt Gateway to the needs of Metropolitan State by following the vision statement of becoming “an accessible one-stop service center that educates and enables students to self-serve and reach their educational goals.”

In addition to those mentioned above, associates include Beakal Elala, Amiin Harun and Tamara Merkouris, all dedicated to enhancing services for students and community awareness of Gateway.
