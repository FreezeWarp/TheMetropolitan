Determination and focus

Linda A. Williams DECEMBER 2010

With winter graduation imminent for many seniors at Metropolitan State University, the grim Minnesota state unemployment rate of seven percent as of September 2010 may impede the joy of the holiday season for several students looking for jobs.

If such is the case for you, do not allow the economic situation to cause dismay to settle on your turf. Job opportunities are still available to those who seek employment with an innovative approach.

Jocelyn Stone, a Metropolitan State student and editor-in-chief of The Metropolitan newspaper used creativity, wit and tenacity to land a job as associate editor of Experience Life magazine. Stone said she was determined and focused to secure this position.

In the midst of her elation, I asked Stone how she positioned herself to obtain her new job—which happens to be her dream job. She said she first got out of her comfort zone. Next she built the confidence within herself to pursue the position. Then she set out with a specific agenda.

What Stone did to get her career started is what all Metropolitan State students are taught to do. Don’t just think persistence and flexibility; try them.

According to Susan Perry, an instructor at Metropolitan State, perseverance is necessary to attain jobs. And Kathy Graves, also an instructor at Metropolitan State, puts emphasis on the need to be resilient when seeking job opportunities.

Keep these ideas in mind when creating your job-seeking plan of action:

1. When you get out of your comfort zone, you are assessing your skills. Decide to spread your wings and take your skills to the next level by applying them in spite of your fears.
2. When you build your self-confidence to pursue your goals, you are saying to yourself, I can do this. Then do it. Start with a plan that includes tasks to execute your goals.
3. When you start taking on tasks to pursue your goals, you are reinforcing the determination to accomplish your goals.

For more information about jobs and careers, visit the Bureau of Labor Statistics at www.bls.gov/oco/ and Metropolitan State Career Services at www.metrostate.edu/career/
